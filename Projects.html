Discovery Project

-

As an exploration of PCB design for ECE 1100, I completed a two-part discovery project with a design component and a hands-on component.

>> Design Stage | Eagle PCB

Central to designing a PCB is the CAD software used to select parts and create a layout. Upon the recommendation of other ECE majors and my peer leader from ECE 1100, I decided to introduce myself to Eagle PCB using online tutorials with the goal of completing a simple PCB.

After an overview of the basics of Eagle, I decided to make use of the soldering training board designed by Robojackets, which I would later physically solder at the Hive, to practice designing something in Eagle without a 1:1 video tutorial.

>> Hands-On Stage | PCB Soldering Training

The hands-on stage of my project involved a Robojackets Soldering Training session at Georgia Tech's Interdisciplinary Design Commons, an ECE makespace run by The Hive, a student organization. As I hope to become a member of The Hive myself, I decided to make use of this training opportunity to become familiar with some of The Hive's resources.
